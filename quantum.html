Qubits

This is a quantum bit also known as a qubit. This qubit can exist in a state of either 0 or 1 as well as a mysterious mix of both. These states can also be thought of as off/on, true/false, low/high, etc. just the same as classical, but with the introduction of quantum logic gates these qubits start to behave in a very weird manner. A qubit represents a single unit of information just like a classical bit, but the representation of this information is much different. Representing information as qubits can make highly complex computations possible and allow you to do things a classical computer cannot. As you move on through the next few pages you'll realize how different qubits are from classical bits.
